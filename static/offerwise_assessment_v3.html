05 — Billion-Dollar Lens
What Still Separates OfferWise from Scale

Every billion-dollar consumer software company has network effects and repeat purchase. Zillow gets better with more listings. Airbnb gets better with more hosts. OfferWise, right now, has neither — but the path to both is visible and specific.

The structural problem is identical to yesterday: one home purchase equals one analysis, then the buyer closes and disappears for seven years. The consumer product is the hook. The B2B channel — inspector, agent, brokerage — is the engine. The repeat purchase mechanism is OfferWatch as a long-term subscription, not 45 days.

Already has
Differentiated AI product
Cross-reference engine, state-aware legal context, 11 agentic features, OpenAI fallback. No direct competitor does all three. This is the product foundation.
Already has
B2B distribution channel
Inspector → buyer pipeline structurally correct, partially instrumented. Champion inspector outreach is the only action needed. Most consumer RE startups never find this channel.
Already has
5 revenue lines
Buyer credits, subscriptions, inspector pro, contractor marketplace, B2B API. Most startups at this stage have one. The question is which to double down on first.
Already has
Data moat starting to accumulate
QuickFeedback buttons now wired. Every analysis completion with a labeled repair outcome ("found" / "not found") is a training example no competitor can buy.
Critical gap
Network effects
Zero. Each user's experience is independent of every other user. No shared comps, no crowd-sourced repair cost validation, no "X buyers analyzed this property." This is the ceiling.
Critical gap
Repeat purchase mechanism
One home purchase = one analysis = done. OfferWatch as a 7-year post-close subscription ("Property Intelligence") is the structural fix — not a 45-day trial.
Path forward
OfferWatch post-close
$4.99–$9/mo monitoring permit activity, market comps, and maintenance alerts after close. Converts a 45-day window into a 7-year subscriber relationship.
Path forward
Inspector champion program
5 inspectors × 20 reports/month = 1,200 buyer acquisitions/year at near-zero CAC. Run the SQL, email the top 10 personally this week. This is the single highest-leverage action available.
Framework observation
"The most dangerous thing about OfferWise's current position is that the product is genuinely excellent and the market is real — which makes it easy to mistake product quality for business model validity. Product quality is necessary but not sufficient. The first paying customer is the only signal that matters right now."
