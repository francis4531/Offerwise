01 · The Problem

A market running on
institutional blindness.

The residential real estate transaction is a $2.8 trillion annual market with a stunning information asymmetry baked into its structure. Sellers know what they’re hiding. Buyers don’t know what to look for. Every professional in between is operating on experience and intuition — not structured data.

Buyer’s problem
11 min

Time spent reading the inspection

The average buyer commits to a $420,000 purchase in 72 hours based on an 80-page inspection report they spend ten or fifteen minutes reading. No framework to evaluate it. No way to cross-reference what the seller claimed. No data to calculate what it actually costs to fix.

Inspector’s problem
$0

Value captured from findings

Home inspectors document everything — and their expertise ends the moment the PDF is sent. Their findings never become financial leverage. Their business is commoditized on Yelp because buyers can’t see the difference between a thorough inspector and a checkbox inspector.

Agent’s problem
2M+

Agents with no data tool

There are over 2 million licensed real estate agents in the US. None of them have a product that turns a property’s actual documents into a specific, defensible offer number in under 90 seconds. They guide buyers on instinct while Zillow estimates erode their credibility.

02 · The Innovation

A calibrated hybrid system.
Not a chatbot wrapper.

OfferWise is not a single prompt to a foundation model. It is an orchestrated pipeline of document parsing, rules-based cross-reference, calibrated ML models, and Claude reasoning — with our native ML overriding the LLM only when the model’s confidence clears a 0.85 threshold. The architecture means adding ML can only make outputs better, never worse. Full engineering detail at /architecture.

The orchestrated pipeline

A single Claude call would produce a generic response. Instead, OfferWise runs a multi-stage pipeline — document parser, cross-reference engine, repair cost predictor, Property Risk DNA, transparency scorer, predictive engine, and the offer architect — each with a defined role and a defined output. The intelligence orchestrator coordinates them; calibrated XGBoost models override Claude on cost and classification when they outperform it.

The result is not a summary. It is a decision: three offer scenarios with rationales, repair cost ranges grounded in real public-records and contractor pricing data, and contradictions surfaced with page-level citations. Typical analysis run time is fifteen to twenty-five seconds.

Separately, thirteen municipal data crawlers run nightly against public records portals, feeding labeled findings into the 121K-row training corpus that the daily ML training agent retrains against — with automatic regression rollback. This is what creates a data flywheel a foundation model alone cannot replicate.

Data Compounding

121K-row labeled corpus, daily retraining

The training corpus has reached 121K labeled findings via a mix of public records crawlers (13 active municipal portals) and customer-analysis-derived labels. Daily retraining with calibrated regression rollback. A competitor would need to rebuild crawler infrastructure across many cities and accumulate equivalent volume — not impossible, but takes meaningful time.

Network Effects (candidate)

Four-persona architecture

Buyer, inspector, agent, and contractor code surfaces are all shipped with cross-persona notification hooks. The network effect this would produce is structural — switching means losing the network — once volume crosses adoption thresholds in multiple personas. Today, the only persona with paying customers is the buyer. The other three are the work ahead.

Document Intelligence

AI-first document parsing

Reads scanned inspection reports, multi-format disclosures, and specialty inspection PDFs via Claude as the primary parser with regex as safety net — the inverted architecture that catches semantic findings rules-only parsing misses. Specialized problem refined over many production releases.

Regulatory Positioning

Designed to complement, not replace

Explicitly stays within decision-support software — partnering with inspectors and agents rather than disintermediating them. Crawlers respect robots.txt and only collect public-records data. Real-estate-specific regulatory posture that pure-LLM competitors with no domain knowledge will navigate poorly.

Distribution Channel

Inspectors as low-CAC channel

The inspector product is built: every analysis generated by an inspector carries their branding and a footer credit to OfferWise. InterNACHI alone has roughly 30K member inspectors. If even single-digit-percent penetration activates, each inspector becomes a recurring buyer-discovery surface at zero marginal CAC. Today this channel has minimal volume; the build is done and the activation is the work.
