01 · The Problem

A market running on
institutional blindness.

The residential real estate transaction is a $2.8 trillion annual market with a stunning information asymmetry baked into its structure. Sellers know what they’re hiding. Buyers don’t know what to look for. Every professional in between is operating on experience and intuition — not structured data.

Buyer’s problem
$38–67K

Hidden cost per transaction

The average buyer commits to a $420,000 purchase in 72 hours based on an 80-page inspection report they spend 11 minutes reading. No framework to evaluate it. No way to cross-reference what the seller claimed. No data to calculate what it actually costs to fix.

Inspector’s problem
$0

Value captured from findings

Home inspectors document everything — and their expertise ends the moment the PDF is sent. Their findings never become financial leverage. Their business is commoditized on Yelp because buyers can’t see the difference between a thorough inspector and a checkbox inspector.

Agent’s problem
2M+

Agents with no data tool

There are over 2 million licensed real estate agents in the US. None of them have a product that turns a property’s actual documents into a specific, defensible offer number in under 90 seconds. They guide buyers on instinct while Zillow estimates erode their credibility.

02 · The Innovation

Six patent-pending breakthroughs.
One unified system.

OfferWise is not a chatbot wrapper. It is a purpose-built agentic architecture that deploys 13 specialized AI agents simultaneously against two documents — the inspection report and the seller disclosure — and synthesizes their outputs into a complete offer strategy. Nothing like it exists.

The agentic pipeline

Where a single AI model produces a generic response, OfferWise deploys a team of specialists — each with a defined role, a defined data source, and a defined output. The agents run in parallel, then the Offer Architect synthesizes everything into three offer tiers and a ready-to-send addendum.

The result is not a summary. It is a decision. This architecture is why OfferWise can cross-reference a seller’s written disclosure against an inspector’s findings and surface the specific page, the specific claim, and the specific dollar consequence — in under 10 seconds.

This is what separates OfferWise from every “AI for real estate” product that came before. Those products answer questions. OfferWise takes a position.

What no one else has built.

01
Patent Pending

OfferScore™

A single investability grade (0–100) computed from 50+ property factors — structural integrity, systems condition, environmental risk, market position, and disclosure honesty. The first standardized score for a property’s actual condition at the moment of purchase. Think of it as a credit score for real estate decisions.
02
Patent Pending

Property Risk DNA™

A visual risk fingerprint covering 12 risk categories simultaneously: structural, mechanical, electrical, plumbing, environmental, seismic, wildfire, flood, financial, legal, disclosure, and market. No other product maps a property’s complete risk profile across all dimensions in a single view.
03
Patent Pending

Seller Transparency Report™

The only product that systematically cross-references every claim in a seller’s written disclosure against every finding in the inspection report — surfacing contradictions with page-level citations. Not keyword matching. Semantic cross-reference against structured document graphs.
04
Novel Architecture

13-Agent Parallel Analysis

13 purpose-built agents in parallel — each with a specialized role, a specific data source, and a structured output. The Offer Architect synthesizes all 13 outputs into three tiered offer recommendations. Results in under 10 seconds. Structurally reliable rather than probabilistically useful.
05
Novel Architecture

OfferWatch™ — Autonomous Monitoring

After analysis, OfferWatch deploys an autonomous agent that watches the property daily — alerting the buyer if the price drops (recalculating the offer), an earthquake occurs nearby (updating the seismic report), new permits are filed (flagging undisclosed work), or comparable sales shift market position.
