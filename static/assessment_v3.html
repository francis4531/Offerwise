02 — Analysis
The Billion-Dollar Lens

In 24 hours, OfferWise added Facebook and Nextdoor as live GTM channels, fixed a single-vendor AI dependency that would have taken the product offline during any Anthropic outage, and rebuilt the conversion funnel so users see the product working before they're asked to sign up or pay. These are real, meaningful improvements.

And yet the grade doesn't move. That's not a failure of execution — it's a function of what the grades actually measure. Technical improvements and GTM infrastructure don't change the PMF grade until someone pays. Distribution grades don't move until a channel converts. The work done this session builds the conditions for a grade change, but doesn't trigger one.

Assessment note

"The most important number in the business right now is not the OfferScore, not the route count, and not the test pass rate. It is the number of people who typed a real address into /analyze and clicked 'Get my free analysis.' That number is the only leading indicator that matters this week."

— Framework observation, v3

The billion-dollar readiness score moves from 35% to 38% — a small but legitimate increment. The fallback architecture means the product can survive an Anthropic outage. The GTM pipeline means content is being generated across five channels daily. The /analyze page means ad traffic now lands somewhere designed to convert. These are infrastructure improvements, not revenue events. The score moves when customers do.

The structural tension in the business remains unchanged from v2: the product is excellent; the distribution is unproven; the LTV is weak. The path to a higher score runs through three things that require no more code: (1) email the top 5 inspectors personally this week, (2) read the first CAC data when it arrives in the next 24 hours and act on it immediately, (3) post the first Facebook and Nextdoor content manually today. Everything else is maintenance.
